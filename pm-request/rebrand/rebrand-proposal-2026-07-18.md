# Complete rebrand proposal

**Product today:** Private Room  
**Recommended new brand:** **Ownfold**  
**Date:** 18 July 2026  
**Decision:** Rebrand around ownership, not secrecy.

---

## The short answer

Rename the product **Ownfold**.

Use this main line:

> **Your work. Your AI. One private file.**

Use this short explanation:

> Ownfold keeps your files, chats, memory, and AI work inside one encrypted file on your Mac. No account. Cloud only when you choose it.

The new brand should feel calm, warm, exact, and professional. It should not look like a hacker tool. It should not look like another purple AI chat app. It should not use a lock, shield, robot, brain, cloud, or sparkle as its main icon.

The main icon should be two pieces of paper folding inward. Together, they make a simple **O**. The open space in the middle can also look like a page or doorway.

My recommended budget is **$22,000–$38,000 in cash**, plus 8–12 weeks of focused founder and engineering time. If all design and engineering are hired out, plan for **$45,000–$80,000**. Do not spend $100,000+ on an agency at this stage.

Do not change the file extension until the name passes a real trademark search.

---

## What this product really is

This is not mainly a chatbot.

It is a private work container that the user owns.

One encrypted file contains:

- normal files, including PDFs, Word files, sheets, code, images, audio, and video;
- chats and AI memory;
- saved versions and recovery information;
- local search, OCR, transcription, and AI actions;
- a local AI that can open, edit, mark, and create files;
- exact source receipts that take the user back to the real words in a file;
- optional cloud and outside-tool access, clearly controlled by the user.

The strongest product idea is this:

> The workspace is a document. The user can hold it, copy it, back it up, AirDrop it, and keep opening it even if the company disappears.

The second strongest idea is this:

> The AI does not only talk next to the files. It works inside them.

The third strongest idea is this:

> Important answers can point to exact, checked words in the source.

These three ideas should lead the whole brand.

Privacy still matters. But “private AI” is now a crowded message. [AnythingLLM](https://anythingllm.com/), [Jan](https://www.jan.ai/docs), and [LM Studio](https://lmstudio.ai/app-privacy) all lead with local or private AI. Ownfold must say something they cannot easily copy:

> **A complete AI workspace inside one encrypted file that belongs to you.**

## Who should care first

The first paying audience should be people who work with sensitive files and need proof.

### 1. Solo lawyers and small law firms

The simple pitch is:

> One case. One encrypted file. Every important answer can show its source.

This audience values ownership, privilege, version history, exact quotes, and a clear line between local and cloud.

### 2. Therapists in private practice

The simple pitch is:

> Notes and recordings stay on this Mac unless you choose otherwise.

This audience values local transcription, simple templates, recovery, and calm language. Legal health claims must be reviewed before use.

### 3. Journalists, researchers, and NGOs

The simple pitch is:

> Search, transcribe, compare, and question sensitive material without putting it into someone else’s cloud.

This group may pay less, but its trust and public support can help the brand.

The product can later serve many other people. The first brand should still be broad enough for personal research, journals, and private projects.

## What is wrong with the current brand

The current work is already consistent. The problem is not poor taste. The problem is that the brand tells only part of the story.

### The name is too generic

“Private Room” explains privacy, but not ownership, proof, action, or the one-file idea. It is hard to own in search. It can also sound like a hotel room, hospital room, chat room, or adult service.

### The keyhole is familiar, but ordinary

The current keyhole and doorway say “locked.” Many password, VPN, vault, and security products use the same idea. The product is more interesting than a lock.

### Dark violet says “AI security app”

The current dark ink and violet are polished, but the market is full of dark purple AI products. The result feels more technical and secretive than calm and useful.

### The product feels denser than the brand promise

The live product has files, a large document viewer, chat, memory, models, recording, workflows, a room map, and a long settings area. The small type and dark surfaces make the product feel harder than it is.

### Some feature names add too many metaphors

“Room,” “Seal,” “Closet,” “Leash,” “Studio,” “Map,” and “AI advisors” all ask the user to learn a new idea. A brand can have one strong metaphor. It should not make every setting part of that metaphor.

## Recommended name: Ownfold

Pronounce it **OWN-fold**.

### Why “Own”

The user owns the file.

There is no required account. There is no company server holding the workspace. The user chooses where the file lives, how it is backed up, and when anything leaves the Mac.

“Own” is the business promise.

### Why “fold”

Files, chats, memory, versions, and AI work are folded into one portable file.

“Fold” also gives the brand a useful visual idea. Pages can fold open when the workspace opens and fold inward when it locks. This is calmer and more original than a glowing keyhole.

“Fold” is the product architecture.

### Why the whole name works

Ownfold joins the emotional value and the technical value:

- **Own** = it belongs to you.
- **Fold** = everything is inside one file.

It is not named after AI. That is good. Models will change. Ownership should not.

### Risks

- The name is invented. The brand must show the pronunciation once during launch.
- Some people may first hear “one fold.” Clear visual spelling will fix this.
- `ownfold.com` was registered in June 2026 and did not answer during this review. `ownfold.app` appeared unregistered in an RDAP check on 18 July 2026. Domain status can change at any time. [ICANN explains that RDAP is the current system for domain registration data](https://www.icann.org/rdap/).
- A normal web search did not find an active software product using the exact name, but that is not a legal search.

### Required legal step

Before public use, pay a trademark lawyer to search the United States, European Union, United Kingdom, Israel, WIPO records, app stores, company records, and confusingly similar names.

The US government fee starts at **$350 for each class** when the application meets the base rules. Legal work and other countries cost extra. [USPTO fee information](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/trademark-fee-information).

Do not rename `.roomai`, the CLI, or the open format until this search is complete.

## Other name ideas

These are useful because they show the different possible positions. They are not all safe to use.

| Name | What it says | Good part | Problem | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ownfold** | Ownership + one file | Best match to the real product | Needs legal search; `.com` is registered | **Recommended** |
| **Quietfold** | Calm + containment | Beautiful visual and emotional idea | Already used by an acoustic-door product and other online products | Reject |
| **Proofroom** | Evidence + protected work | Strong for lawyers and researchers | An active AI hiring product already uses ProofRoom | Reject |
| **Unshared** | Radical privacy | Very easy to understand | An active private-photo product already uses it; message is too negative | Reject |
| **Trove** | A valuable personal collection | Warm and friendly | Very crowded, including several AI knowledge products | Reject |
| **Within** | Everything stays inside a boundary | Calm and broad | Common word, crowded name, weak ownership | Backup idea only |
| **Private Room** | Privacy + place | No migration cost | Generic and does not carry the unique story | Keep only as a temporary bridge |

The lesson is simple: names built from “vault,” “haven,” “keep,” “room,” “local,” and “private” are crowded. A made name with a clear story gives this product a better chance.

## Three full brand directions

### Direction A — Ownfold: warm precision

**This is the recommended direction.**

It balances professional trust with human warmth.

- Main idea: “This work belongs to you.”
- Main line: “Your work. Your AI. One private file.”
- Look: warm paper, dark green ink, mineral teal, small copper proof marks.
- Icon: two paper ribbons folding into an O.
- Voice: plain, calm, exact.
- Best for: lawyers, therapists, journalists, researchers, and personal work.
- Risk: the new name needs teaching and legal clearance.

### Direction B — Evidence first

This direction makes source proof the hero.

- Main idea: “Do not trust the answer. Check it.”
- Main line: “AI work with receipts.”
- Look: white, deep blue, red source marks, strong document typography.
- Icon: an exact quote held between two brackets.
- Best for: legal work, investigations, compliance, and research.
- Risk: it makes the product feel narrow. It hides the one-file ownership story.

Use this as a campaign or professional landing page, not as the whole company brand.

### Direction C — Radical privacy

This direction makes “nothing leaves” the hero.

- Main idea: “No account. No server. No telemetry.”
- Main line: “The AI workspace no one else holds.”
- Look: black, white, one safety color, strong technical proof.
- Icon: a closed boundary with one user-controlled door.
- Best for: security experts, journalists, activists, and launch attention.
- Risk: it can feel fearful, cold, and defensive. It also places the product in direct competition with every local-AI app.

Use this for trust pages and technical launch content, not as the main emotional brand.

## The recommended visual system

See the [brand board](ownfold-brand-board.svg), [app icon](ownfold-app-icon.svg), and [interface direction](ownfold-ui-direction.svg).

### The app icon

Use a mark called **The Fold**.

Two paper ribbons turn inward. Together, they make an O. The open center suggests a page, a doorway, or a protected space.

The icon should:

- work at 16, 32, 128, and 1024 pixels;
- have one clear shape, not fine detail;
- use no letters at small sizes;
- have no lock, keyhole, shield, robot, brain, sparkle, or cloud;
- have a matching document icon for workspace files;
- keep a simple one-color version for menus, print, and source receipts.

Use Apple system icons only inside the app, not as the company mark. Apple’s software agreement says system images cannot be used as app icons or trademarks. [Apple system-image terms](https://www.apple.com/legal/sla/docs/xcode.pdf).

### Color

The product should support both light and dark mode. Light mode should become the default because people spend time reading documents.

| Role | Color | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#14201E` | Main text, dark mode, app tile |
| Paper | `#F5F1E8` | Main light background |
| Mineral | `#3C8D83` | Main actions, selected state |
| Sea glass | `#8BC7BA` | Soft focus, local status, secondary highlight |
| Receipt | `#C87941` | Verified sources and clear online-data warnings |
| White | `#FFFDF8` | Cards and reading surface |

Green should not automatically mean “safe.” It should mean “local or complete.” Copper should mark proof or a decision that can send data out.

### Type

- Use **SF Pro** or the normal macOS system font inside the product.
- Use a custom-drawn lowercase wordmark for “ownfold.”
- Use one warm serif only on the website and launch pages, not for controls.
- Make normal app text at least 14–15 px. Do not use tiny all-cap labels for important actions.
- Keep line lengths short in settings and trust messages.

### Icons

Use one icon family:

- 18 or 20 px grid;
- 1.75–2 px rounded strokes;
- filled color only for selected or dangerous states;
- no emoji in product navigation;
- no decorative sparkles for every AI feature.

Important icon ideas:

| Feature | Icon idea |
|---|---|
| Workspace file | A page with one folded corner |
| Verified source | A short quote inside a page marker |
| Local AI | A small computer with a dot inside it |
| Cloud AI | A cloud with one outward arrow and a written warning |
| Remembered | A bookmark with a small dot |
| Workflows | Three connected steps |
| Recording | A waveform inside a simple circle |
| Connections | Two linked nodes or plugs |
| Lock | A normal small lock, used only for the real lock action |

### Motion

Replace the glowing Seal with a short paper-fold motion.

- Unlock: the two sides move outward and the workspace appears.
- Lock: the two sides fold inward and the screen settles to ink.
- Duration: about 280–380 ms.
- No glow burst.
- No sound.
- Reduced-motion mode changes state at once.

The motion should feel like closing a document, not entering a vault.

### Photography and illustration

Show real work, not cyber security.

Good images:

- a person working with a contract, interview, research note, or case file;
- the same workspace file moving from one Mac to another;
- a source receipt opening the exact line in a document;
- a Mac in airplane mode while transcription and search still work;
- close views of paper, tabs, marks, and clear text.

Avoid hooded people, glowing locks, server racks, purple clouds, robots, and abstract AI brains.

## What the product should look like

Keep the useful three-part structure: files, work, and AI. Make the document feel primary.

### Top bar

- Show the workspace name first.
- Show a clear state such as “Local · stays on this Mac.”
- When cloud is active, use a full written state: “Cloud is on · this question and attached text can leave your Mac.”
- Keep Lock at the far right.
- Do not make users decode a model name before they know whether data leaves the Mac.

### File area

- Use more space between rows.
- Use file-type icons, not the same sparkle for generated files.
- Put Add and Search first.
- Move “Summarize workspace” into Workflows or the home page. It does not need to sit above every file.
- Make Remembered a normal section, not a special glowing card.

### Main work area

- Use a white or warm paper reading surface.
- Keep toolbar actions quiet until needed.
- Give verified source receipts a strong, consistent copper style.
- Make version history feel like a normal document history, not a separate “Time Machine” metaphor.

### AI area

- Rename it “Ask this workspace.”
- Keep the local/cloud state next to the input.
- Let source receipts be easy to scan.
- Keep Attach and Workflows visible.
- Use simple progress steps when the AI acts on files.

### Home page

The current home page has useful cards but too much empty space.

Lead with one helpful next action:

> Continue working on Client case

Then show recent files, recent chats, remembered notes, and workflows. Empty cards should teach the user what to do next.

### Connections map

Replace the large glowing stars with small document cards or dots. Show file names clearly. Use line weight to show strength. The current space-map look is playful, but it does not match legal, therapy, or research work.

### Settings

Reduce the left list from many items to six clear groups:

1. AI
2. Privacy and lock
3. Online and cloud
4. Connected tools
5. Workspace behavior
6. Recovery and storage

Each group should begin with a one-sentence answer to “what can leave this Mac?”

Advanced terms such as Ollama, MCP, model size, and ports should live under an Advanced switch when possible.

## File format and migration

The file is the promise. Do not break it for a rebrand.

### Recommended plan

1. Keep opening every `.roomai` file forever.
2. After legal clearance, let new workspaces use `.ownfold`.
3. Register both extensions with macOS.
4. Keep the internal `roomai` format marker at version 1 until a real format change is needed.
5. Add `ownfold` as the new CLI name, but keep `roomai` as an alias.
6. Do not silently rename a user’s existing file.
7. Offer a simple optional action: “Use the new .ownfold file name.”
8. Keep recovery sidecars working for both extensions.
9. Test double-click open, Finder icons, AirDrop, backup, recovery, duplicate, export, update, and rollback.

This avoids a dangerous situation where the new brand makes old private files feel abandoned.

The code audit found about **253 `Private Room` or `roomai` mentions across 67 files**, plus the app icon, document icon, installer background, banner, badges, screenshots, Tauri bundle data, CLI, sidecar name, updater path, documentation, and test fixtures. This is a real migration, not a find-and-replace job.

## Website and launch message

### Hero

> **Your work. Your AI. One private file.**  
> Ownfold keeps files, chats, memory, and AI work in one encrypted file on your Mac. No account. Cloud only when you choose it.

Buttons:

- Download for Mac
- Watch the 45-second proof

### Three proof points

**Own it**  
Move it, copy it, back it up, or AirDrop it like any other file.

**Check it**  
Answers can open the exact words in the source. If the words are not there, Ownfold does not pretend they are.

**Work inside it**  
The AI can open, mark, edit, compare, transcribe, and create files inside the workspace.

### Trust line

> If Ownfold disappears tomorrow, your work still opens.

### Launch video

Make one 45-second film with no cuts that hide network use.

1. Turn on airplane mode.
2. Open a workspace file.
3. Ask a question about a PDF.
4. Open the exact source receipt.
5. Fix one line in a document.
6. Lock the workspace.
7. Show that it is one file in Finder.

This proves more than a page full of security claims.

## Product price recommendation

The price should support the ownership promise. A forced monthly subscription would weaken the message.

### First, finish trust basics

Keep the product free during the early beta until:

- the app is signed and notarized;
- first model setup is simple;
- recovery and old-file migration have been tested with real users;
- cloud warnings are consistent;
- the name is legally cleared.

The Apple Developer Program costs **$99 per year**. It provides the Developer ID needed for trusted distribution. [Apple membership details](https://developer.apple.com/programs/whats-included/).

### Then charge once

Recommended launch pricing:

- **Personal: $79 one time** — all core privacy, files, chat, local AI, source receipts, export, and recovery.
- **Professional: $149 one time** — business use, recording workflows, advanced connections, and priority support.
- **Optional updates after year one: $39 per year Personal / $69 Professional.** The installed app keeps working if the user does not renew.

Offer an early price of **$59** to the first 500 personal users.

Never put encryption, export, recovery, local mode, or source receipts behind the higher plan. Those are the trust promise. Charge for business use, advanced work, and support.

If offline license checks are needed, use a signed license file that can be stored locally. Do not require a permanent account merely to prove ownership.

## Rollout plan

### Week 1–2: prove the name

- Run the full legal search.
- Check domains, social names, app stores, and company records.
- Test “Ownfold” with 10 target users.
- Ask each person to say the name after hearing it once.
- Buy the chosen domain only after the first legal green light.

### Week 2–4: finish the identity

- Draw the final vector mark.
- Test it at all icon sizes.
- Build light and dark color tokens.
- Finish wordmark, document icon, installer art, and source-receipt style.
- Write the one-page voice guide.

### Week 4–7: change the product

- Add the new tokens and icon family.
- Redesign the top bar, file area, source receipts, home page, map, and settings.
- Replace user-facing product language.
- Keep advanced technical names only where needed.
- Add the new lock/unlock fold motion.

### Week 6–9: protect compatibility

- Support both file extensions.
- Add the new CLI alias.
- Update bundle IDs only if signing and update history allow it safely.
- Test old rooms, recovery sidecars, updater behavior, Finder, backups, and exports.
- Do not change the encrypted format just to match the brand.

### Week 8–10: rebuild every public surface

- Website and download page.
- GitHub name and README.
- Product screenshots and demo room.
- Installer, updater, release notes, feedback form, email, and social names.
- Privacy pledge and format specification.
- Comparison page against local chat apps.

### Week 10–12: launch carefully

- Give existing users a clear “Private Room is now Ownfold” message.
- Explain that old files still work.
- Publish the one-take proof film.
- Publish a simple technical article about the one-file format.
- Watch recovery, open-file, update, and model-install errors closely.

## What not to do

- Do not make the logo another padlock.
- Do not keep dark violet as the whole identity.
- Do not rename every feature with a brand metaphor.
- Do not silently rename user files.
- Do not change encryption or the database format during the visual rebrand.
- Do not claim legal, medical, HIPAA, privilege, or compliance outcomes without review.
- Do not buy an expensive `.com` before the name is legally safe.
- Do not charge a monthly fee for the basic right to keep opening local files.
- Do not lead with model names, parameters, Ollama, MCP, or “agents.” Lead with the work the user owns.

---

## How to know the rebrand worked

Show the new page and app to people who have never seen the product. After five minutes, ask them to explain it.

The rebrand passes if most people say something close to:

> It is an AI workspace inside one encrypted file that I own. It can work on my files and show me where answers came from.

Track these numbers:

- 8–12% of qualified website visitors download the app.
- 60% of new users create or open a workspace and add a file in the first session.
- 70% ask a first useful question in 10 minutes.
- 80% can correctly say when data leaves the Mac.
- 35% return in week four.
- Fewer than 5% of support messages are about the file-name change or old-file access.

If people only say “it is a private chatbot,” the rebrand has failed.

## Final recommendation

Move forward with **Ownfold**, subject to legal clearance.

Use **warm precision** as the design direction.

Make ownership the main message, source proof the main product proof, and privacy the promise underneath both.

Spend first on the name search and final icon. Then redesign the key product surfaces. Keep every old file working. Launch only when the app is signed, recovery is tested, and the local/cloud story is easy for a non-technical person to repeat.

The simplest version of the whole strategy is:

> **Ownfold**  
> **Your work. Your AI. One private file.**
